# Prettier

## Status
Accepted - 6/18/2026

## Reasoning
I am early in my journey to understand the intricacies of coding. As such, I would like to take as much advantage as possible of the knowledge of those more experienced. Prettier is a collective of the experience of those who have coded for much longer than I have and those who have ran into stylizing problems much more than I have. Using prettier will hopefully teach me what "good coding" looks like and present to me different options such that I can make my own judgements on my preferred style.

## Use Case
It is important to note that prettier is used for the reformatting of code into a style that is more agreeable for multiple parties, even if some parts of it are not preferrable for one or multiple parties. Based on my current plan, I want to use it mostly for HTML, but as it seems to work for JS and CSS as well among other languages, I will continue forward using it for those also. 

## Pros
1. Prettier will be a good demonstration of "good" code for me to absorb
2. Prettier will present different styles of code that I am not accustomed to which should help me decide what my preferred style is as well as introduce me to potentially better styles
3. If I bring on more people to work on this project, this will likely make integration much easier (note that since this is a personal project this likely won't happen, but it's a good consideration anyway)
4. Since prettier sees much use in the workplace, I see the merit in learning to use and configure it. While I likely won't be the one to be configuring it in the real world, knowing the configuratons will likely help me work with it more effectively
5. While I am currently working on github, many organizations use their own platforms for git. As such, their integration of something like prettier may look different from my own. While this is unavoidable, it may help to understand their implementations by understanding this implementation first.

## Cons
1. Prettier will invalidate my styles in favor of its own. While this may make my code "better", it will also remove the personality from my code. Good or bad, this will result in the loss of my historical improvement, which would otherwise be fun to look at in the future.
2. Prettier has known heuristics that can cause some inconsistencies, especially with other dependencies, so I should be careful when using it and testing to make sure that nothing is breaking.
